# DOK-1 attention-check MCQs — first 4 checkpoints

Alternate low-floor versions of the four mid-video checkpoints (cues 96.5 s, 181.5 s,
285.5 s, 301.5 s). Each is pure DOK-1 recall of something said or shown in the seconds
immediately before the cue, so a correct answer confirms the student was watching.
Format matches the script's EDITOR INSERT blocks.

## CHECKPOINT 1 — cue 96.5 s (after THE FIVE STAGES)

Prompt: "Where is the climax in Freytag's Pyramid?"
A) At the very beginning, before the action starts
B) ✅ At the top — the peak of the pyramid
C) At the end, where things settle down
D) Outside the pyramid entirely
Explanation: "The climax is the turning point at the peak of the pyramid — in the video it lights up as the lightning strike at the very top."

## CHECKPOINT 2 — cue 181.5 s (after TWO FLAVORS OF CONFLICT)

Prompt: "A sailor fighting to keep his boat from going under is an example of —"
A) internal conflict
B) ✅ external conflict
C) no conflict at all
D) the resolution
Explanation: "The sailor is struggling against something out in the world — the storm and the sea — so the conflict is external, like the figure braced against the storm in the video."

## CHECKPOINT 3 — cue 285.5 s (after THE HABIT)

Prompt: "What does the index card Alex pins to her corkboard say?"
A) "Memorize the pyramid"
B) ✅ "Find the conflict first"
C) "Skip to the climax"
D) "And then what happens?"
Explanation: "Alex pins one card to the corkboard — 'FIND THE CONFLICT FIRST' — and the pyramid snaps into place around it."

## CHECKPOINT 4 — cue 301.5 s (READING CHECKPOINT, Poe excerpt)

Prompt: "In the excerpt on screen, the narrator says he had been 'very, very dreadfully' —"
A) angry
B) tired
C) sleepy
D) ✅ nervous
Explanation: "The excerpt opens: 'TRUE! — nervous — very, very dreadfully nervous I had been, and am…'"
